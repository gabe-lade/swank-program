Jingke Wu is a Ph.D. student in AEDE at The Ohio State University.
His research interests focus on environmental economics, urban and regional economics, and the intersection of land use, housing markets, and environmental policy.
He uses econometric and spatial analysis methods to study the impacts of environmental factors such as concentrated animal feeding operations (CAFOs) and point source pollution on property values and community well-being.
He is skilled in using ArcGIS, ArcPy, and ENVI to analyze both raster and spatial data, using geographic information to assess impacts on regional economic activities.
Outside of research, he enjoys hiking and photography.
