Median home sale price

Median price of valid residential sales each quarter. Through 2025Q2 the series reflects each parcel’s most recent recorded sale (a “last-sale” sample — see notes); from 2025Q3 (dashed line) it reflects complete conveyance records. Click a legend name to hide/show a community.

Is Hilliard becoming less affordable than the region?

Median sale price as a percent of the Franklin County median (100% = same as the county overall). A rising Hilliard line means Hilliard prices are growing faster than the region's.

Income needed to buy the median Hilliard home

Household income required to buy the median-priced Hilliard home without spending more than 30% of income on housing (10% down, prevailing 30-year mortgage rate, plus taxes and insurance), compared with the HUD Area Median Income for the Columbus metro.

Median price per square foot

Normalizes prices across home sizes — useful when the mix of homes sold shifts between quarters.

Typical asking rent

Zillow Observed Rent Index (smoothed, all homes and apartments). Captures what renters face, which sale prices miss. Zillow begins covering smaller cities only once it has enough listings, so some peer lines start mid-series. Data provided by Zillow Group (zillow.com/research/data).

Hilliard sale prices by home type

Median sale price of single-family detached homes vs condos/townhomes in Hilliard. Condos and townhomes are the main lower-cost ownership option; a widening gap means fewer attainable entry points.

Zillow typical home value (ZHVI)

Zillow's smoothed, seasonally adjusted estimate of the typical home value (35th–65th percentile, all homes). An independent cross-check on the auditor sale medians that is not affected by which homes happened to sell in a quarter. Data provided by Zillow Group (zillow.com/research/data).

About this tracker

Draft. All panels are computed from real data except the permits panel, which is an illustrative placeholder pending Building Division data. Sale prices come from arm's-length residential sales recorded by the Franklin County Auditor, aggregated by city staff each quarter. Rents are the Zillow Observed Rent Index (smoothed, all homes and multifamily), a repeat-rent index of asking rents; home values use Zillow's ZHVI from the same portal. Building permits come from City of Hilliard Building Division records (or the Census Building Permits Survey). Mortgage rates are quarterly averages of the Freddie Mac 30-year fixed survey rate. Area Median Income is the HUD income limit for the Columbus metro. The income-needed calculation assumes a 10% down payment, 1.8% effective property tax rate, $1,200/yr insurance, 0.4% PMI on the loan, and a 30% housing-cost-to-income standard — calibrated to match the 2026 Hilliard Housing Study. It is a consistent affordability index over time, not an underwriting estimate for any particular buyer; property tax is the most sensitive assumption.

Method notes: sales through July 14, 2025 come from the Auditor parcel file (each parcel's most recent valid sale; LUC 510–513 and 550–556, price ≥ $10,000, multi-parcel package transfers removed); sales from July 21, 2025 onward come from the Auditor daily conveyance files (non-exempt, land-and-building, single-parcel). Recorded sales for July 15–20, 2025 are not in either file and are omitted (≈6% of 2025Q3 recording days, so that quarter’s counts are slightly understated). Earlier quarters slightly under-count homes that have since resold. Dublin and Westerville figures cover their Franklin County portions only. Hilliard quarterly medians are based on roughly 60–180 sales, so single-quarter moves should be read cautiously; the price-per-square-foot chart helps control for mix shifts. Mortgage rates for 2025Q3 onward and AMI values before 2023 are close estimates pending replacement from the PMMS archive and huduser.gov.

Updating

City staff update three small data tables inside this file each quarter (instructions are in the accompanying explainer document and in comments at the top of this file's code), then repost the file to the city website. No software is required beyond a text editor and a web browser.
